\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced the class of weakly non-separable Hamiltonians and the
Extended-MFGAN algorithm, which extends GAN-based MFG solvers beyond
the separable regime. Our main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A formal definition of weak non-separability and the GAN-tractable
    condition $\eps L_R < \lambda$, answering when GAN methods are superior
    to physics-informed methods.
  \item Extended-MFGAN: a fixed-point GAN iteration provably convergent in
    Wasserstein-2, with explicit rate $\kappa^k = (\eps L_R/\lambda)^k$.
  \item Numerical validation on the MFG-LWR traffic benchmark and
    high-dimensional settings.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Limitations and future work.}
The GAN-tractable condition $\eps L_R < \lambda$ is sufficient but may not be
necessary; closing this gap is an open problem.
For $\eps L_R \geq \lambda$, combining Extended-MFGAN's outer iteration
with MFDGM's inner solver (replacing APAC-Net) could extend applicability —
we leave this hybrid approach to future work.
Extending to \emph{mean field control} (MFC) and applications to
manufacturing flow optimization and multi-agent reinforcement learning
are natural directions.
