Mean Field Games (MFGs) model large populations of rational agents interacting
through a mean-field coupling, and are described by a coupled system of
Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) and Fokker-Planck (FP) equations.
Existing deep learning methods split into two families:
GAN-based approaches (APAC-Net, MFGANs) achieve strong Wasserstein convergence
guarantees but are restricted to Hamiltonians with separable structure;
physics-informed methods (MFDGM) handle non-separable Hamiltonians but sacrifice
the distributional learning advantages of GANs.

We introduce the class of \emph{weakly non-separable Hamiltonians},
defined as perturbations of a separable base by a coupling term of strength $\eps$:
\[
  H(x,\rho,p) = H_0(x,p) - f_0(x,\rho) + \eps\, R(x,\rho,p).
\]
We propose \textbf{Extended-MFGAN}, a fixed-point GAN iteration that solves
MFGs in this class by reducing each outer step to a separable subproblem
solvable by standard GAN methods. We prove that the outer iteration is a contraction
in the Wasserstein-2 metric whenever $\eps \cdot L_R < \lambda$, where $L_R$ is
the Lipschitz constant of $R$ in $\rho$ and $\lambda$ is the monotonicity
constant of $f_0$, yielding linear convergence with rate $(\eps L_R/\lambda)^k$.
This provides the first formal characterization of the regime where GAN methods
outperform physics-informed approaches.
We validate our method on the MFG-LWR traffic flow benchmark, demonstrating
superior convergence and Wasserstein accuracy compared to MFDGM, and recover
APAC-Net exactly in the separable limit $\eps = 0$.

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Mean Field Games, Generative Adversarial Networks,
Non-Separable Hamiltonians, Wasserstein Convergence, Traffic Flow.
