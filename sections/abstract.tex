Existing deep learning solvers for Mean Field Games fall into two families.
GAN-based methods learn population densities distributionally and come with
Wasserstein convergence guarantees, but are restricted to Hamiltonians with
separable structure. Physics-informed methods handle non-separable Hamiltonians
but provide only weak pointwise approximations with no explicit convergence rate.
We introduce the class of \emph{weakly non-separable Hamiltonians}, where the
coupling between density and momentum is measured by a single parameter,
and propose Extended-MFGAN, a fixed-point GAN iteration that handles this class
without abandoning the distributional learning and Wasserstein guarantees of
GAN-based solvers. Our main result is a contraction theorem in the Wasserstein-2
metric, with a geometric convergence rate determined explicitly by the ratio of
coupling strength to Lasry-Lions monotonicity. This provides the first rigorous
criterion separating the regimes where GAN methods outperform physics-informed
approaches and vice versa, and the first explicit convergence rate for any deep
learning MFG solver in the non-separable setting. We further prove that the
condition is sharp via an explicit divergence counterexample, and validate the
contraction rate numerically on the MFG-LWR traffic flow benchmark, recovering
the predicted phase transitions to four-digit accuracy.

\noindent\textit{Keywords:} Mean Field Games, Generative Adversarial Networks,

Traffic Flow.
