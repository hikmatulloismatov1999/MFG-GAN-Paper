\section{Numerical Validation of the Linearised Rate}
\label{sec:experiments}

\paragraph{Scope.}
The numerical results in this section validate the \emph{linearised}
contraction rate proved in \Cref{prop:bilinear_exact} and
\Cref{prop:necessity}.  Concretely, we iterate the analytically
derived linearisation of the outer fixed-point map $\mathcal{T}$ at the
uniform equilibrium and compare the empirical contraction with the
theoretical rates described below.

\paragraph{What is \emph{not} done here.}
We do \emph{not} train an APAC-Net inner solver, nor solve the full
nonlinear PDE pipeline of \Cref{alg:main} end-to-end.  A full
neural-network implementation of the inner GAN solver, and a
pipeline-level benchmark against MFDGM, are left to future work
(\Cref{sec:conclusion}).  The present experiments are deliberately
narrow: they target settings where the linearized outer map is explicit
and validate the predicted rates to four-digit accuracy.

\paragraph{Setting.}
All experiments use the ergodic MFG on the torus
$\Omega = \mathbb{T}$ with $f_0(\rho) = \lambda\rho$ and $\nu = 0$.
Experiments~1 (separable limit) and 3 (phase transition) use
the density-only coupling $R(x,\rho) = L_R\rho$ with
$H_0(p) = \tfrac{1}{2}p^2$;
Experiment~2 uses the parameterised bilinear traffic family
\eqref{eq:Heps}, with $H_1$ the original MFG-LWR Hamiltonian.
For each problem, the linearised outer map is diagonal in Fourier modes;
its operator norm is $\kappa = \eps L_R/\lambda$ for the density-only
case and $\kappa = \eps|1-\eps|/\lambda$ for the parameterised bilinear
traffic case around the no-drift equilibrium (\Cref{prop:bilinear_exact}).
This is the prediction the experiments test.

\subsection{Setup}

We discretise the torus with $N_x = 64$ spatial points and parameterise the
outer fixed-point map $\mathcal{T}$ by its action on the linearised
perturbation $\delta\rho = \rho - \rho^*$ around the uniform equilibrium
$\rho^* \equiv 1$.
On the torus, $\mathcal{T}$ is diagonal in Fourier modes; for the $k$-th
mode the linearised map acts as
\[
  \widehat{\delta\rho}_k \;\longmapsto\;
  \frac{\varepsilon\, L_R}{\lambda + \nu^2 (2\pi k)^2/\lambda}\,
  \widehat{\delta\bar\rho}_k.
\]
For $\nu = 0$ this reduces exactly to the density-only rate
$\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$.
We initialise with $\delta\rho^0(x) = 0.05\cos(2\pi x)$ and iterate
the outer map $K=12$ times.

\subsection{Separable limit (Experiment 1)}

We first verify the separable-limit observation in \Cref{sec:algorithm}: when
$\varepsilon = 0$ the effective Hamiltonian $H^k = H_0$ is independent
of $\bar\rho$, so the outer fixed-point map is constant and the wrapper
reduces to one separable inner solve.

For $\varepsilon \in \{0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5\}$ we apply the linearised outer
map to the initial perturbation $\delta\rho^0(x) = 0.2\cos(2\pi x)$
and record the error after one and after eight iterations.

\subsection{Bilinear traffic flow (Experiment 2)}

We next validate the local-rate calculation of Proposition~\ref{prop:bilinear_exact}
on the MFG-LWR traffic Hamiltonian (\Cref{sec:traffic})
$H(x,\rho,p) = \tfrac{1}{2}p^2 - (1-\rho)p$, with bilinear coupling
$R(x,\rho,p) = \rho p$ and $\varepsilon = 1$ in the decomposition
\eqref{eq:decomp}.
The no-drift uniform equilibrium is $(\rho^*, p^*) = (1, 1-\varepsilon)$,
at which $L_R(p^*) = |p^*| = |1-\varepsilon|$.
The linearised analysis (analogue of Proposition~\ref{prop:bilinear_exact})
gives the contraction rate
\[
  \kappa = \frac{\varepsilon\, |1-\varepsilon|}{\lambda}.
\]
For $\lambda = 0.2$, the equation $\kappa = 1$ has two roots in $(0,1)$,
$\varepsilon_\pm^* = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 \pm \sqrt{1 - 4\lambda}) \approx \{0.276,\, 0.724\}$,
producing two phase transitions.

The non-monotonicity reflects the structure of the bilinear coupling:
the effective Lipschitz constant $L_R(p^*) = |1-\varepsilon|$ vanishes
exactly at $\varepsilon = 1$, so the system becomes contractive again
for $\varepsilon$ close to $1$.  This is a feature of the MFG-LWR model
that follows directly from Proposition~\ref{prop:bilinear_exact}.

\subsection{Phase transition (Experiment 3)}

We sweep $\varepsilon \in \{0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5\}$
with $\lambda = L_R = 1$, so the GAN-tractable threshold is $\varepsilon^* = 1$.
Table~\ref{tab:phase_transition} reports, for each $\varepsilon$,
the theoretical contraction rate $\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$,
the empirical contraction rate $\hat\kappa$ measured as the median of
consecutive ratios $\|\rho^{k+1}-\rho^*\|/\|\rho^k-\rho^*\|$,
and the final error $\|\rho^{12}-\rho^*\|_{L^2}$.

The match is exact for this linearized density-only sweep: empirical and theoretical rates
agree to four digits, and the transition between geometric decay
($\varepsilon < 1$) and geometric growth ($\varepsilon > 1$) occurs precisely
at $\varepsilon^* = 1$.
This is direct empirical evidence for the sharpness of the density-only
weak-coupling condition.

\subsection{Discussion}

The exact agreement between $\hat\kappa$ and the theoretical rate in the
linearized tests is a consequence of
two facts established in the paper.
First, \Cref{prop:bilinear_exact} shows that the linearised outer map at the
uniform equilibrium has an explicitly computable operator norm, reducing to
$\varepsilon L_R/\lambda$ in the density-only sweep and to
$\varepsilon|1-\varepsilon|/\lambda$ for the parameterised traffic family.
Second, in the density-only family, \Cref{prop:necessity} shows that the
rate persists through the iteration:
$\|\rho^k - \rho^*\|_{L^2}$ scales as
$\kappa^k \|\rho^0 - \rho^*\|_{L^2}$ for all $k$.
The numerical experiments confirm these predictions to four-digit
accuracy in the linearised regimes tested here.

Across the three experiments the empirical contraction rate matches the
theoretical prediction to four decimal places, in regimes ranging from
the trivial separable limit ($\varepsilon = 0$, Experiment~1) through
the parameterised bilinear traffic family with two phase transitions
(Experiment~2) to the density-only sweep with a single sharp threshold
(Experiment~3).
In the linearised regime, this confirms the central
analytical claim of the paper: in the density-only subcase, the
weak-coupling condition $\varepsilon L_R < \lambda$ is necessary and
sufficient for geometric convergence, with rate exactly
$\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$.
Whether the rate persists at the same value in the fully nonlinear
PDE regime, beyond a neighbourhood of the uniform equilibrium, is an
open question.

A pipeline-level comparison against MFDGM, a full neural-network
implementation of the inner GAN solver, and a high-dimensional scaling
study are natural extensions left for future work.
