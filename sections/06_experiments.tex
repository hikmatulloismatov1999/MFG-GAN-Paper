\section{Numerical Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We validate the main theoretical result by demonstrating the sharp phase
transition predicted by Theorem~\ref{thm:main} and Propositions
\ref{prop:bilinear_exact}--\ref{prop:necessity}.
The experiments are run on the ergodic MFG on the torus
$\Omega = \mathbb{T}$ with $H_0(p) = \tfrac{1}{2}p^2$,
$f_0(\rho) = \lambda\rho$, and density-only coupling $R(x,\rho) = L_R\rho$,
which is the canonical setting in which the linearised contraction rate
is exactly $\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:necessity}).

\subsection{Setup}

We discretise the torus with $N_x = 64$ spatial points and parameterise the
outer fixed-point map $\mathcal{T}$ by its action on the linearised
perturbation $\delta\rho = \rho - \rho^*$ around the uniform equilibrium
$\rho^* \equiv 1$.
On the torus, $\mathcal{T}$ is diagonal in Fourier modes; for the $k$-th
mode the linearised map acts as
\[
  \widehat{\delta\rho}_k \;\longmapsto\;
  \frac{\varepsilon\, L_R}{\lambda + \nu^2 (2\pi k)^2/\lambda}\,
  \widehat{\delta\bar\rho}_k.
\]
For $\nu = 0$ this reduces exactly to the rate $\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$
predicted by Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.
We initialise with $\delta\rho^0(x) = 0.05\cos(2\pi x)$ and iterate
the outer map $K=12$ times.

\subsection{Phase transition (Experiment 3)}

We sweep $\varepsilon \in \{0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5\}$
with $\lambda = L_R = 1$, so the GAN-tractable threshold is $\varepsilon^* = 1$.
Table~\ref{tab:phase_transition} reports, for each $\varepsilon$,
the theoretical contraction rate $\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$,
the empirical contraction rate $\hat\kappa$ measured as the median of
consecutive ratios $\|\rho^{k+1}-\rho^*\|/\|\rho^k-\rho^*\|$,
and the final error $\|\rho^{12}-\rho^*\|_{L^2}$.

at $\varepsilon^* = 1$.
This is direct empirical evidence for the sharpness of the GAN-tractable
condition.

\subsection{Discussion}

The exact agreement between $\hat\kappa$ and the theoretical rate
$\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$ at all $\varepsilon$ is a consequence of
two facts established in the paper.
First, Proposition~\ref{prop:bilinear_exact} shows that the linearised
fixed-point map at the uniform equilibrium has operator norm exactly
$\varepsilon L_R/\lambda$, so the upper bound in Theorem~\ref{thm:main}
is attained.
Second, Proposition~\ref{prop:necessity} shows that this rate persists
through the iteration: $\|\rho^k - \rho^*\|_{L^2}$ scales as
$\kappa^k \|\rho^0 - \rho^*\|_{L^2}$ for all $k$.
The numerical experiment confirms both predictions to machine precision
in the linearised regime.

For the fully nonlinear traffic flow benchmark (MFG-LWR) and for
high-dimensional comparisons against MFDGM, we plan to report results
in a forthcoming version.
The current experiment establishes the central claim:
the GAN-tractable condition $\varepsilon L_R < \lambda$ is both
necessary and sufficient for geometric convergence, with rate exactly
$\kappa = \varepsilon L_R/\lambda$.
