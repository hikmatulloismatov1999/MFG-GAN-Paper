Dear Prof. Gomes,

I have put together a short note (attached) summarising the current state of the
project on extending GAN-based solvers for Mean Field Games to weakly non-separable
Hamiltonians. Rather than sending the full manuscript, I thought it would be more
useful at this stage to share a four-page overview covering:

- the main idea and the proposed fixed-point algorithm,
- the contraction theorem and a short outline of its proof,
- a sharpness check on the bilinear traffic-flow family,
- the numerical experiments I have completed so far,
- a list of limitations I am aware of, and a few specific questions on which I
  would value your guidance.

The note also explains, in a short remark, the role of the parameter ε and why I
use the word "weakly" in the title — the algorithm itself is well defined for any
non-separable Hamiltonian, but the smallness condition is what guarantees that
the outer iteration is a contraction.

Before moving toward any formal submission, I would very much like to hear what
you think of the proof, the assumptions, and the publication strategy. I have
listed five specific questions at the end of the note, but please feel free to
comment freely on anything that stands out.

The full draft, code, and slides are also on GitHub for reference, in case you
prefer to look at the longer manuscript:
https://github.com/hikmatulloismatov1999/MFG-GAN-Paper

I would be happy to meet to discuss any of this at a time that suits you.

Thank you very much for your time and feedback.

Best regards,
Hikmatullo Ismatov
CEMSE, KAUST
